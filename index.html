Questions parents ask

Do you write the essay for my student?

No — and that protects your student. Omar coaches brainstorming, structure, and revision, but the words and the voice stay the student's. Admissions offices are good at detecting ghostwritten essays, and an authentic voice is precisely what gets students admitted. The goal is a student who owns their story, on the page and in every interview after.

When should we start?

The summer before senior year. The Common App opens August 1, and the strongest personal statements are drafted before the school-year crunch begins. Starting in October means competing with homework, deadlines, and panic — starting in July means time to find the right story.

What exactly is included in each package?

Essay Sprint covers the Common App personal statement from brainstorm through unlimited drafts. Full Narrative covers the personal statement, supplemental essays for up to six schools, and the activities list, unified into one coherent story. Hourly advising handles anything targeted — a single essay, a school list review, or interview prep.

Do you work with graduate school applicants?

Yes. Undergraduate applications are the focus, but Omar advises graduate and professional school applicants using the same narrative-first approach that earned his own MPH admission.

How does the pro bono program work?

A portion of each cycle's caseload is reserved at no fee for students from under-resourced high schools, and 10% of all advising revenue funds essay prep, tutoring, and after-school programs. If cost is the barrier, reach out anyway — a compelling story never pays a fee.
